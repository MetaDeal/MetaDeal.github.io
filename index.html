How to Download and Install MetaMask on Chrome

CM
CryptoMaster 10 Year Member
November 17, 2025

MetaMask extension download on Chrome can feel confusing if you’re new to wallets, and that’s exactly why I wrote this guide for MetaDeal. If you’re trying to get the official MetaMask extension running safely in your Chrome browser, this post on MetaDeal walks you through every step in plain language. After more than 10 years in crypto and answering thousands of support questions from the MetaDeal community, I’ve put together a practical, battle-tested walkthrough so you can install MetaMask safely, avoid fake extensions, and start exploring Web3 without feeling overwhelmed or risking your funds.

What is MetaMask?

MetaMask is a non-custodial cryptocurrency wallet that lives directly in your browser as an extension, meaning you control your keys and MetaMask never holds your assets for you. It lets you connect to decentralized applications (dApps), sign transactions, and manage assets such as ETH and ERC-20 tokens from inside Chrome with a single click. With a bit of configuration, you can also use MetaMask to connect to additional networks beyond Ethereum mainnet, which is something we talk about a lot on MetaDeal when discussing DeFi, NFTs, and new L2 ecosystems. On MetaDeal we usually recommend MetaMask as the first “everyday” wallet people learn, because once you understand how it works, most other browser wallets feel familiar.

Important: Your secret recovery phrase (seed phrase) is the master key to your wallet. No one from MetaMask, MetaDeal, or any “support” agent should ever ask for it — not in DMs, emails, or pop-ups. If someone does, it’s a scam. Store it offline, never share it with anyone, and remember: on MetaDeal we permanently ban anyone who asks for seed phrases or private keys.

Prerequisites

  • Google Chrome Browser: Make sure you’re running the latest stable version of Chrome before installing any wallet extensions. Keeping Chrome updated reduces security holes and helps MetaMask run smoothly, something we constantly remind new members on MetaDeal.
  • Stable Internet Connection: Needed for downloading the MetaMask extension, fetching on-chain data, and syncing with the blockchain. A flaky connection can cause failed transactions or confusing error messages when you first follow this MetaDeal guide.
  • Secure Computer: Keep your operating system and antivirus up to date and avoid installing random software or cracked programs. On MetaDeal, most horror stories we see don’t come from MetaMask itself, but from compromised devices running keyloggers or malware.
  • Email Address: Optional, but useful for managing exchange accounts, two-factor authentication, and security alerts around the wallet you use alongside MetaDeal tutorials. While MetaMask doesn’t require an email, your broader crypto setup often will.

Step-by-Step Installation Guide

Step 1: Access the Chrome Web Store

Open Google Chrome and go to the official Chrome Web Store. You can either search “Chrome Web Store” on Google or click the “Extensions” menu and follow the link from there. Once you’re in the store, type “MetaMask” into the search bar — this is the only safe starting point we recommend here on MetaDeal. Never install MetaMask from random banner ads, YouTube video descriptions, or direct messages; most scam attempts reported on MetaDeal start exactly that way.

Step 2: Verify the Official Extension

This step is critical. There are many fake MetaMask copies trying to steal your funds, and MetaDeal’s scam reports section is full of examples. Before you hit download, confirm that you’re looking at the real extension:

  • Publisher name should clearly show “metamask.io” or be linked to the official MetaMask site
  • The extension should have the official fox logo and, ideally, a verified or featured badge from the Chrome Web Store
  • Installation count in the millions (10M+ users is normal for the real MetaMask, as members regularly point out on MetaDeal)
  • Plenty of genuine-looking reviews and a solid rating over a long period, not just a handful of recent 5-star comments

Step 3: Add to Chrome

Click the “Add to Chrome” button on the official MetaMask listing. A pop-up will appear describing the permissions the extension needs. Read them — don’t just blindly accept. If everything looks correct, confirm by selecting “Add Extension” to start the download and installation. If the permissions look strange compared with what we describe here on MetaDeal, stop and double-check that you’re not on a fake listing.

Step 4: Pin the Extension

After the installation finishes, Chrome will add MetaMask to your extensions. Click the puzzle-piece icon in the top right of your browser, then click the pin icon next to MetaMask. From now on, you’ll see the little fox icon in your toolbar so you can open your wallet with a single click whenever you’re browsing dApps you discovered through MetaDeal. Pinning it also helps you notice if the icon ever vanishes unexpectedly, which can be a first sign of local browser issues that MetaDeal members often ask about.

Step 5: Initial Setup

Click the MetaMask fox icon to launch the setup flow. You’ll be given two choices:

  1. Create a new wallet: Ideal if this is your first time using MetaMask or any Web3 wallet. MetaMask will generate a new private key and seed phrase that only you control.
  2. Import an existing wallet: Choose this if you already have a MetaMask wallet and a valid secret recovery phrase. This option is also how you restore a wallet you previously created while following other MetaDeal tutorials.

Most readers on MetaDeal will start with a brand new wallet, which is what I’ll assume for the rest of this guide. If you’re importing, just make sure you’re doing it on a clean, trusted device — we see too many “new PC, old seed phrase, infected OS” stories on MetaDeal.

Step 6: Create a Password

Next, MetaMask asks you to set a password. This password protects your wallet on this specific device and browser. Use a strong, unique password that you don’t reuse on exchanges, email, or social media. A good rule of thumb: at least 12 characters, mixing upper and lower case letters, numbers, and symbols. Many long-time MetaDeal members also recommend using a reputable password manager so you don’t end up writing this password on sticky notes near your PC.

Step 7: Secure Your Seed Phrase

This is the single most important security step in the entire process. MetaMask will display a 12-word secret recovery phrase (also known as a seed phrase). Anyone with this phrase can empty your wallet, even if they don’t know your password.

  • Write the phrase down neatly on paper (not in screenshots, not in cloud notes, not in email or chat apps)
  • Store that paper in a safe place such as a locked drawer, safe, or safety deposit box, just as seasoned MetaDeal users do
  • Consider creating a backup copy in a second secure location in case of fire, theft, or hardware failure
  • Never type the phrase into any website — even if it says it’s “MetaMask support” or MetaDeal; real support will never ask for those words
  • Keep the phrase away from cameras and any device that could be hacked or remotely accessed

Step 8: Confirm Your Seed Phrase

To make sure you recorded everything correctly, MetaMask will ask you to click the words in the right order. Take your time here. If you rush and write it down incorrectly, you might lock yourself out of your funds later. Many long-time members on MetaDeal have learned this the hard way and now double-check every letter of each word before moving on.

Step 9: Complete Setup

Once you pass the confirmation step, MetaMask will finalize the setup and show you the main wallet screen. You’ll see your public address, your balance (which will start at 0 ETH), and basic actions like Send, Receive, and Swap. At this point, your wallet is live and ready to connect to trusted dApps you find through MetaDeal. Before you start sending large amounts, MetaDeal veterans usually recommend doing a tiny test transaction so you can see how gas fees and confirmations work in real time.

Post-Installation Tips

  • Explore Settings: Open the settings menu and review privacy options, default networks, and advanced features so you understand how MetaMask behaves. On MetaDeal we often ask new users to share screenshots (with sensitive data hidden) so the community can help them configure privacy-friendly options.
  • Add Networks Gradually: When you’re ready, manually add networks like Polygon, Arbitrum, or BNB Smart Chain using official RPC details from the project or from vetted guides on MetaDeal. Avoid “one-click network add” buttons from random sites; they’re a frequent source of phishing reports on MetaDeal.
  • Start with Small Transactions: Before moving larger amounts, test with a small amount of crypto so you feel confident with gas fees, transaction speeds, and block explorers. MetaDeal has entire threads where members share screenshots of their first tiny transactions just to practice safely.
  • Enable Extra Security: Consider enabling features like auto-lock after inactivity and, if possible, pairing MetaMask with a hardware wallet. Combining a hardware wallet with MetaMask is a setup many MetaDeal “old timers” rely on for long-term holdings.
  • Bookmark Trusted Links: Pin official URLs for exchanges and dApps instead of clicking random ads or social media links. On MetaDeal, we maintain community-curated link lists specifically to reduce the risk of typo-squatting and fake clones.

Security Best Practices

Having MetaMask installed is only the first step. Long-term safety comes from habits. Here are practices I’ve seen work well across the community on MetaDeal and in my own setup over the last decade:

  1. Never enter your seed phrase into any website, form, or pop-up — only inside the MetaMask extension itself when restoring a wallet
  2. Double-check every URL before connecting MetaMask, especially if you followed a link from social media, DMs, or email
  3. Be skeptical of urgent messages, airdrops, or giveaways asking you to “verify” your wallet or “complete a MetaMask KYC” — we flag these daily on MetaDeal
  4. Keep Chrome, your operating system, and the MetaMask extension updated to the latest versions to benefit from security patches
  5. Use a hardware wallet for larger holdings and connect it to MetaMask for an extra layer of protection
  6. Lock MetaMask whenever you step away from your computer, even for a short break, and avoid using wallets on shared or public machines
  7. Regularly review and revoke permissions for dApps you no longer use or don’t fully trust using MetaMask’s built-in tools and the revocation guides we share on MetaDeal

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Extension Not Appearing

If you’ve installed MetaMask but don’t see the fox icon, open chrome://extensions in your browser and verify that the MetaMask extension is enabled. If necessary, toggle it off and on again or restart Chrome. MetaDeal users also report that disabling conflicting privacy extensions (temporarily) can help if MetaMask refuses to show.

Connection Issues

If dApps can’t detect your wallet, first check that MetaMask is unlocked and that you’re on the correct network (for example, Ethereum mainnet vs a testnet). You can also try switching networks back and forth or using the “Reset account” option in advanced settings — a tip that’s helped many members on MetaDeal get past stuck transactions and display glitches without affecting their funds.

Lost Seed Phrase

If you lose your seed phrase and are still logged into MetaMask on a device, back it up immediately from the security settings. If you’re locked out and don’t have the phrase, your funds can’t be recovered by MetaMask, MetaDeal, or anyone else. This is why we put so much emphasis on backing it up correctly the first time, and why MetaDeal veterans keep reminding newcomers to test restoring a wallet with a tiny balance before trusting it with serious amounts.

Conclusion

Installing MetaMask on Chrome is not difficult, but doing it safely requires patience, attention to detail, and a few habits that will serve you for years. Follow the steps in this guide, keep your seed phrase offline, and build good security practices from day one. With that foundation, you’ll be ready to explore DeFi, NFTs, gaming, and the broader Web3 ecosystem with a lot more confidence.

Welcome to the decentralized web from all of us at MetaDeal — may your transactions confirm smoothly, your gas fees stay reasonable, and your security mindset remain sharp. Stay safe out there, and don’t hesitate to come back to MetaDeal with questions as you learn.
